Millenial Writing and GitHub

Story of poor teenage rebellion written by the daughter of the world's 5th richest man

I kind of realize now that a lot of my writing is hyper-self-aware, and that seems to be a trait of millenial writing or redditor dialog. You don't have to be constantly hiding behind layers of irony and an inability to be unaware. People having a dialog don't always instantly think of witty gotchas and retorts to one up each other. Conversations are not a game of word tennis or anything.

There is a certain balance that needs to be considered between realism and other factors like comedic effect or exposition, just like how there's a balance between being super self-aware and having a lack of self-awareness. For example, this game everyone is hating on, Mixtape, has a lot of irony surrounding it because it is supposed to be this coming of age story with these rebellious teenagers who were dealt slightly worse hands in their lives compared to others, yet it was written by the daughter of the CEO of fucking Oracle. Like I doubt she's ever had any hardships when it comes to money or privilege. This doesn't mean you can't write about it (that's the beauty of fiction writing), but she lacks a lot of genuine experience with this subject matter for sure.

Another example is this Mouse PI for hire game. I remember when I first saw a trailer for it, I assumed it was an indie game made by like one person or maybe a small studio. Turns out it was what's called a "AA" game, where it has the size and direction of an indie studio with the budget of a AAA studio. It's bullshit, because it can have the community hype and charm of an indie game (a lot of people seem to like certain games just because they're indie) and yet it has the advertising, outreach, and funding of major studios. I learned it has Troy Baker and Debra Wilson as voice actors in the game, which it's safe to say that they probably aren't doing charity work when it comes to the roles they play.

The gameplay seems ok, but the writing in the game is strange. Everyone has to constantly make quips, many of the characters seem more like caricatures, and there's cheese jokes that are milked to death (I know it's a game about mice and mice like cheese, but holy shit you don't have to shoehorn it into every other line.) I've never written a game script so I guess I don't have the best experience to backup my opinion, but man when the game is set in the 1930s and breaks the fourth wall to make a joke about sigmas, that's just bad writing.
